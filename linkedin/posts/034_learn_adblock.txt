🚀 Exploring AdGuard DNS: An Interesting Alternative to Traditional Ad Blockers

I’ve never been a supporter of browser-based ad-block extensions. But after installing a new OS recently, I decided to experiment with AdGuard DNS as a simple way to filter unwanted content at the network level.

After a month of using it, I have to admit—it's a very interesting approach. It keeps things clean without needing any extensions, and the browsing experience feels noticeably lighter.

However, I’ve run into an unexpected issue:
Some websites detect that I’m “using an ad blocker” and block access to their content… even though I’m not actually running any ad-block extension.

💡 This raises a question:
If I don’t have an ad-blocking feature enabled, but DNS filtering prevents certain elements from loading, is the result effectively the same as traditional ad blocking?

Curious to hear how others think about DNS filtering vs. dedicated ad blockers.
Is DNS-level filtering becoming the new “silent ad blocker”?
