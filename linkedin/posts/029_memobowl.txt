After sharing my thoughts about information overload recently, I started exploring tools and technologies that approach this problem from different angles.

There are some really interesting projects in the knowledge / AI space right now:

• Obsidian – personal knowledge graphs and linked notes
• NotebookLM – AI-assisted research and document understanding, which is very powerful, especially with `notebooklm-py` – an unofficial Python API
• Logseq – open-source PKM with graph linking
• Tana – AI-native knowledge graph
• Heptabase – whiteboard + knowledge graph
• Mem – AI organizes notes automatically

Each of these solves a piece of the puzzle.

What I find fascinating is how these ideas might combine into something that helps people manage knowledge more intelligently — not just store more information.

Still exploring and experimenting.

If you're working with tools in this space, I’d love to hear what you’re using.
