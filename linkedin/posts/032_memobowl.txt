I’ve been thinking about information overload — not just in terms of volume, but in terms of novelty.

How much of what we save is actually new?

To explore this, I mapped my personal “region of interest.”

I aggregated saved LinkedIn posts, articles, bookmarks, and newsletters, then:
• decomposed the content into smaller semantic units
• converted them into embeddings
• clustered similar ideas
• projected the structure into a 3D space

The outcome was consistent and difficult to ignore:
• A large proportion of content significantly overlaps
• Many ideas recur across different sources with minimal variation
• Truly novel information is comparatively sparse

This suggests that the challenge is not only filtering *relevant* information, but identifying what is *genuinely additive* to what we already know.
